Vocabulary List

My favorite vocab words!

Aberration

noun

a departure from what is normal, usual, or expected, typically one that is unwelcome.:"they described the outbreak of violence in the area as an aberration" "I see these activities as some kind of mental aberration" "the decade was seen as a period of aberration in the country's progress towards a democratic society".

Absquatulate

verb

Leave abruptly. Example: "Some overthrown dictator who had absquatulated to the U.S.A."

Acculturate

verb

Assimilate or cause to assimilate a different culture, typically the dominant one. Examples: "Those who have acculturated to the US", "The next weeks were spent acculturating the field staff", "An acculturated Cherokee."

Apocryphal

adjective

(of a story or statement) of doubtful authenticity, although widely circulated as being true. Examples: "There is an apocryphal story about a disgraced rock star who ended up in bankruptcy court", "Many remarks were attributed to him, most of them probably apocryphal."

Belligerent

adjective

Hostile and aggressive. Example: "A bull-necked, belligerent old man."

noun

A nation or person engaged in war or conflict, as recognized by international law. Example: "Ships and goods captured at sea by a belligerent."

Deleterious

adjective

Causing harm or damage. Example: "Divorce is assumed to have deleterious effects on children."

Homeopathy

noun

The treatment of disease by minute doses of natural substances that in a healthy person would produce symptoms of disease.

Indefatigable

adjective

(of a person or their efforts) persisting tirelessly. Example: "An indefatigable defender of human rights."

Languid

adjective

(of a person, manner, or gesture) displaying or having a disinclination for physical exertion or effort; slow and relaxed. Example: "They turned with languid movements from back to front so as to tan evenly."

Weak or faint from illness or fatigue. Example: "She was pale, languid, and weak, as if she had delivered a child."

Macabre

adjective

Disturbing and horrifying because of involvement with or depiction of death and injury. Example: "A macabre series of murders."

Mellifluous

adjective

(of a voice or words) sweet or musical; pleasant to hear. Example: "The voice was mellifluous and smooth."

Nadir

noun

the lowest point in the fortunes of a person or organization.: "they had reached the nadir of their sufferings".

Nascent

adjective

(especially of a process or organization) just coming into existence and beginning to display signs of future potential. Example: "The nascent space industry."

Pulchritudinous

adjective

Beautiful. Example: "Dan gazed admiringly at the pulchritudinous brunette."

Pusillanimous

adjective

Showing a lack of courage or determination; timid.

Sagacious

adjective

Having or showing keen mental discernment and good judgment; shrewd. Example: "They were sagacious enough to avoid any outright confrontation."

Sequacious

adjective

(of a person) lacking independence or originality of thought.

Sesquipedalian

adjective

(of a word) polysyllabic; long. Example: "Sesquipedalian surnames."

Verisimilitude

noun

The appearance of being true or real. Example: "The detail gives the novel some verisimilitude."

Vexatious

adjective

Causing or tending to cause annoyance, frustration, or worry. Example: "The vexatious questions posed by software copyrights."
